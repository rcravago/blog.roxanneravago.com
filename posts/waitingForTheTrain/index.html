waiting for the train

a poem on Love

you’re standing on the platform
behind the yellow line
waiting for the train to come.

you look at your watch.
it’s 10:55.
you pace back and forth.
your right shoulder starts hurting
so you sling your bag to the other side.

you look at your watch again.
it feels like eternity
but only a minute has passed.
it’s 10:56.

you lean over the edge of the platform
and peer into the darkness in the distance.

in the darkness
you see a glimmer of light.
you say to yourself,
finally.

you walk back behind the yellow line
and wait for the train to come.
people start to crowd around you
pushing and shoving.

drawing closer and closer
that glimmer of light
now getting bigger and bigger.
a figure of a train appears.

but it doesn’t stop.
there’s no one on board.
“Out of Service” it says on the train.

this feeling starts to swell inside you.
it feels horrible and uncomfortable.
and you can’t control it.
or at least you think you can’t.
the feeling of anxiety.
now when will the next train come?

behind the yellow line
you wait
again.

you hear over the loudspeaker
trains are delayed due to train traffic.
you look at the screen overhead
the next train will be arriving in 10 minutes.

you look at your watch again.
it’s 11.
should I wait hear?
or leave and take a different train?
maybe I should just take a taxi?
it’ll probably be faster.
but I don’t want to spend that money.
then again I don’t want to wait any longer.
what if the train is further delayed?
damnit, I’ll just wait.

before you even realize
a sudden gust of wind blows
from behind your back
and throws you off guard.
it’s 11:11.

you turn around
and the train just arrived.
this time you wait patiently
as passengers get off.
when it’s clear
you get on the train
take your seat
and smile to yourself
because all that time you spent worrying
it didn’t matter.
it didn’t matter at all.

all you needed to do
was wait for the right one to arrive.
